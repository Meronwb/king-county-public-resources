Mapping Access to Public Resources in King County

This project explores the spatial distribution of key public resources across King County, Washington — including public health clinics, community centers, libraries, and parks. By visualizing where these resources are located, we aim to identify patterns of accessibility and potential gaps in service across neighborhoods.

Access to public resources plays an important role in community well-being, education, public health, and overall quality of life. This project uses interactive web maps to highlight how access to these resources varies across different areas of King County.
